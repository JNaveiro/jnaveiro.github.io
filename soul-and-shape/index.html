Totally geodesic submanifolds

  • $M$ real analytic Riemannian manifold, $f\colon \Sigma \to M$ isometric immersion.
  • $f$ is totally geodesic if:

    • $\gamma\colon I\to \Sigma$ geodesic $\Rightarrow$ $f\circ \gamma \colon I \to M$ geodesic.

$\mathbb{R}^{k}\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n}$

$\mathsf{S}^{k}\subseteq\mathsf{S}^{n}$

$\mathbb{R}\mathsf{H}^{k}\subseteq\mathbb{R}\mathsf{H}^{n}$

General problem

  • $f\colon \Sigma^k \to M^n$ is compatible if $\widetilde{f}\colon \Sigma\to \mathsf{Gr}_{k}(TM)$ given by \[\widetilde{f}(x)=df_x(T_x\Sigma)\] is injective.
  • $f$ admits a unique inextendable extension.
  • $f_i\colon \Sigma_i\to M$ ($i=1,2$) are equivalent (and we write $f_1\simeq f_2$) if there exists an isometry $\phi\colon \Sigma_1\to\Sigma_2$ with $f_1=f_2\circ\phi$.
Given $M$, classify (equivalence classes of) inextendable compatible totally geodesic immersions to $M$ up to congruence.

Riemannian homogeneous spaces

  • $M=\mathsf{G}/\mathsf{K}$ Riemannian homogeneous space, $o=e\mathsf{K}$.
  • $\mathsf{G}\curvearrowright M$ gives a homomorphism $\mathsf{G}\to I(M)$.
  • Anti-homomorphism $X\in \mathfrak{g}\mapsto X^*\in \mathfrak{X}(M)$: \[ X^*_p = \frac{d}{dt}\bigg\vert_{t=0} \operatorname{Exp}(tX)\cdot p. \]
$M$ is reductive if there exists $\mathfrak{p}\subseteq \mathfrak{g}$ with \[ \mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{k}\oplus \mathfrak{p}, \quad \operatorname{Ad}(\mathsf{K})\mathfrak{p}=\mathfrak{p}. \]

Riemannian homogeneous spaces

$M$ is reductive if there exists $\mathfrak{p}\subseteq \mathfrak{g}$ with \[ \mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{k}\oplus \mathfrak{p}, \quad \operatorname{Ad}(\mathsf{K})\mathfrak{p}=\mathfrak{p}. \]
  • $\mathfrak{p}=\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{k}=T_o M$ as $\mathsf{K}$-modules.
  • Isotropy representation $\mathsf{K}\curvearrowright T_o M$ $\leftrightsquigarrow$ Adjoint action $\mathsf{K}\curvearrowright \mathfrak{p}$.
  • $\mathsf{G}$-invariant tensors on $M$ $\leftrightsquigarrow$ $\operatorname{Ad}(\mathsf{K})$-invariant tensors on $\mathfrak{p}$.

Connection and curvature

  • $M$ has a canonical connection $\nabla^c$. \[ (\nabla^c_{X^*}Y^*)_o = -[X,Y]_\mathfrak{p}, \quad X,\,Y\in \mathfrak{p}. \]
  • $D=\nabla-\nabla^c$ difference tensor.
\[ \begin{align} D_X Y &= \frac{1}{2}[X,Y]_{\mathfrak{p}}+U(X,Y), \\[0.5em] 2 \langle U(X,Y),Z\rangle &= \langle [Z,X]_{\mathfrak{p}},Y \rangle + \langle X,[Z,Y]_{\mathfrak{p}} \rangle, \\[0.5em] (\nabla_{X^*}Y^*)_o &= -[X,Y]_{\mathfrak{p}}+D_X Y, \\[0.5em] R(X,Y) &= [D_X,D_Y]-D_{[X,Y]_{\mathfrak{p}}}-\operatorname{ad}([X,Y]_{\mathfrak{k}}). \end{align} \]

Natural reductivity

  • $M$ is naturally reductive if $U=0$.
  • $M$ is naturally reductive $\Leftrightarrow$ $\operatorname{Exp}(tX)\cdot o$ geodesic for all $X\in \mathfrak{p}$.
  • $M$ is normal homogeneous if:
    1. $\mathsf{G}$ has a bi-invariant metric.
    2. The complement $\mathfrak{p}=\mathfrak{g}\ominus \mathfrak{k}$.
    3. The metric on $M$ is induced from the metric on $\mathsf{G}$.

Totally geodesic subspaces

  • $f\colon \Sigma\to M$ totally geodesic. Then $f$ is determined by any tangent subspace $V\in\widetilde{f}(\Sigma):$

    • $f_i\colon\Sigma_i\to M,\quad\widetilde{f_1}(\Sigma_1)\cap\widetilde{f_2}(\Sigma_2)\neq\varnothing\Rightarrow f_1\simeq f_2.$

  • Given $V\subseteq T_pM$, when does $V$ generate a totally geodesic submanifold?
Theorem (Cartan '51, Hermann '59). $M$ real analytic, $V\subseteq T_pM.$ The following are equivalent:
  1. $V$ is a totally geodesic subspace.
  2. $V$ is $\nabla^kR$-invariant for all $k\geq0$.

Totally geodesic submanifolds of $\mathsf{G}/\mathsf{K}$

  • $M=\mathsf{G}/\mathsf{K}$ naturally reductive homogeneous space.
  • Totally geodesic submanifolds through $o$ $\Leftrightarrow$ totally geodesic subspaces of $\mathfrak{p}$.
Tojo's criterion. $M$ naturally reductive, $\mathfrak{v}\subseteq \mathfrak{p}$. The following are equivalent:
  1. $\mathfrak{v}$ is a totally geodesic subspace.
  2. The subspace $e^{D_X}\mathfrak{v}$ is $R$-invariant for all $X\in \mathfrak{v}$.

$D$-invariant totally geodesic submanifolds

  • $M=\mathsf{G}/\mathsf{K}$ naturally reductive, $\Sigma\to M$ submanifold.
  • $\Sigma$ is $D$-invariant if $T_x\Sigma$ is $D$-invariant for all $x\in\Sigma$.
Theorem (LN, Rodríguez-Vázquez). $\mathfrak{v}\subseteq \mathfrak{p}$ subspace. The following are equivalent:
  1. $\mathfrak{v}$ is invariant under $R$ and $D$.
  2. $\mathfrak{v}$ is invariant under $R^c$ and $D$.
  3. There exists a $D$-invariant t.g. $\Sigma \to M$ with $T_o\Sigma = \mathfrak{v}$.
The corresponding t.g. submanifold is $\Sigma = \mathsf{S}\cdot o$, where $\mathfrak{s}=[\mathfrak{v},\mathfrak{v}]+\mathfrak{v}$.

Riemannian cones

  • $(M,g)$ complete real analytic Riemannian manifold.
  • Riemmanian cone: $\widehat{M}=(0,\infty)\times M$ with the metric

    • $\widehat{g}=dr^{2}+r^{2}g.$

  • $I(\widehat{M})=I(M)$.
  • If $\dim M = 6$, and $\mathrm{Ric}=5g$, then (Bär '93):

    • $M$ is strict nearly Kähler $\Rightarrow$ $\mathrm{Hol}(\widehat{M})\in \{0,\mathsf{G}_{2}\}$.

  • If $M\neq \mathsf{S}^{6}$ and $\pi_1(M)=0$, then $\mathrm{Hol}(\widehat{M})=\mathsf{G}_{2}$.
Theorem (LN, Rodríguez-Vázquez). $\Sigma$ totally geodesic in $\widehat{M}$. Then one of the two holds:
  1. $\Sigma=\widehat{S}$ for $S\to M$ totally geodesic.
  2. $\Sigma$ is (up to surjective local isometry) a totally geodesic hypersurface in $\widehat{S}$ for $S\to M$ totally geodesic.

$\Sigma=\widehat{S}$

$\Sigma\subseteq\widehat{S}$

Corollary. $\Sigma \to \widehat{M}$ maximal totally geodesic submanifold. Then one of the two holds:
  1. $\Sigma=\widehat{S}$ for a maximal totally geodesic $S\to M$.
  2. $\Sigma$ is a complete totally geodesic hypersurface.
Example of a type ii submanifold. Take $M=(0,\pi/2)\times \mathbb{R}^2$ with the metric \[ dx^2+(\sin x)^2 p(y,z)^2 dy^2+(\sin x)^2 q(y,z)^2 dz^2 \] The hypersurface \[ \Sigma = \Big\{(\sec x,x,y,z)\colon (x,y,z)\in M\Big\} \subseteq \widehat{M} \] is totally geodesic.

The case ii. is not possible if $\mathrm{Hol}(\widehat{M})=\mathsf{G}_2$ (Jentsch, Moroianu, Semmelmann, '13).

Theorem (LN, Rodríguez-Vázquez). Let $\widehat{M}\neq \widehat{\mathsf{S}^6}$ be a cohomogeneity one $\mathsf{G}_{2}$-cone. Every maximal totally geodesic submanifold of $\widehat{M}$ is the cone of a maximal totally geodesic submanifold of $M$.
Theorem (LN, Rodríguez-Vázquez). Let $\widehat{M}\neq \widehat{\mathsf{S}^6}$ be a cohomogeneity one $\mathsf{G}_{2}$-cone. Every maximal totally geodesic submanifold $\Sigma$ of $\widehat{M}$ is
  1. Associative (i.e. calibrated by the $\mathsf{G}_{2}$-structure $\phi$) if $\dim \Sigma = 3$.
  2. Coassociative (i.e. calibrated by $\star\phi$) if $\dim \Sigma = 4$.
